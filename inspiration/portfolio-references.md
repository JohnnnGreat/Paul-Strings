# Portfolio References — Paul Ojoko

Curated portfolio examples to study before designing. Each has a note on *what specifically to borrow*.

---

## Tier 1 — Must Study (Directly Relevant)

### Musician Portfolios

**Rémi Jousselme — Classical Guitarist**
- Notable: Unique hover interactions where images transform into shutter formation
- Sticky banner linking SoundCloud, Facebook, YouTube
- What to borrow: The image-hover interaction concept; guitarist-first design hierarchy

**Justin Kleiner — Musician**
- Notable: Minimalist, direct links to all streaming platforms in hero
- Embedded Vimeo video in hero section
- What to borrow: Streaming platform integration pattern; hero video embed approach

**Brend Rock — Musician**
- Notable: Dark color scheme (black + white), compelling performance photography above the fold
- What to borrow: The "stage lighting on dark" photography treatment

**Stephen Elsinger — Musician**
- Notable: Embedded audio players that don't interrupt UX
- Subtle hover effects, tactile design sensibility
- What to borrow: The seamless audio player integration

**Global Hypersynth (Pixpa)**
- Notable: Colorful homepage; music videos + quick links to ALL streaming platforms
- Has merch section; good for musician-as-entrepreneur
- What to borrow: The streaming platforms quick-link block layout

**Trooko — Musician**
- Notable: Sleek minimalist design; clean discography grid with clickable album covers
- Direct links to Spotify
- What to borrow: Discography grid pattern

**Visual Cinnamon (Nadieh Bremer)**

**Naledi — Data Analyst**

- Each project highlights a specific skill
- What to borrow: The storytelling-first structure; narrative before portfolio

**Maggie — Data Analyst (GitHub Pages)**

- What to borrow: Project organization; case study card format

## Tier 2 — Study for Craft & Aesthetics

### Award-Winning Creative Developer Portfolios

**Bruno Simon — bruno-simon.com**
- Awwwards Site of the Year 2025 (8.84 rating)
- Notable: Entire portfolio is a 3D interactive driving experience
- Built with: Three.js + WebGL
- What to borrow: The idea of ONE memorable interactive hook (not the full 3D — too heavy); the playful attitude toward navigation
- Warning: Don't copy the complexity, just the fearlessness

**Eduard Bodak — "Built to Move"**
- Featured on Codrops 2025
- Notable: Complex multi-step GSAP animations; animation as primary design element
- What to borrow: The principle that enter/exit animations tell a story; GSAP ScrollTrigger usage patterns

**Samsy Ninja (SMSY)**
- 50+ international awards (Cannes Lions, Awwwards)
- Notable: 3D interactive graphics, computational design
- What to borrow: The intersection of technical precision + artistic vision — that's Paul's brand too

**Jason Bergh — Director of Photography**
- Notable: Video backgrounds, cinematic parallax scrolling
- What to borrow: Cinematic hero section with overlay text; parallax depth

---

### Creative-Technical Dual-Identity Portfolios

**Karim Saab — Art Director + Frontend Developer**
- Stack: Webflow + GSAP + WebGL + Barba.js
- Notable: Moves fluidly across both design and development roles in one site
- What to borrow: How he frames the dual identity as strength; the "what I do" section structure

**The Anthology (DEV Community — nk2552003)**
- Notable: 2026 portfolio with cinematic visual effects, AI-generated images, GSAP ScrollTrigger
- Featured sections: ambient background sounds, custom cursor, typewriter effect
- What to borrow: The ambient audio concept (ties in perfectly with Paul's musician side)
